Lawn Scarifying in Cornwall

Strip out moss and thatch — unlock your lawn's full potential

From £70

If your lawn feels spongy underfoot, looks patchy or is being taken over by moss, it almost certainly needs scarifying. Scarification is the single most impactful treatment you can give a Cornish lawn — it removes the suffocating layer of moss, thatch and dead organic material that builds up over time, allowing air, water and nutrients to reach the roots where they're needed most.

Cornwall's damp, maritime climate is a paradise for moss. High rainfall, mild winters and shaded gardens create the perfect conditions for moss and thatch to accumulate rapidly, often forming a dense mat several centimetres thick. Left untreated, this layer starves grass roots of oxygen, traps moisture against the soil surface and creates an environment where disease thrives. Most lawns in Truro, Falmouth, St Austell and across Cornwall benefit hugely from regular scarification.

We use professional powered scarifiers — not garden rakes — that cut through the thatch layer effectively and consistently across the entire lawn. The process is thorough: we make multiple passes at calibrated depths, collecting vast amounts of dead material (you'll be amazed how much comes out). While your lawn may look a little rough immediately after treatment, the transformation over the following weeks is dramatic — thicker, greener, healthier grass that's far more resilient.

For the best results, scarifying is ideally carried out in autumn (September–October) or spring (March–April) when grass is actively growing and can recover quickly. We also offer follow-up overseeding and top dressing to fill any bare patches and give your lawn the best possible head start. Combined with a regular lawn treatment programme, scarification turns tired, mossy lawns into the lush green spaces they should be.
